Commentary

Development of the Digital Artifact

Research and Content – everyone has been assigned a part to work on, consisting of a certain aspect of the festival, to be introduced as a case study and explained, and a relevant topic of theory to apply to said case study and analyse it critically. Everyone provided their references in a proper format. To prepare ourselves for evaluating the event and analysing it critically, the whole group has read parts of two chapters of the ‘Critical Event Studies’ (Lamond and Platt, 2016): ‘Approaches to research’ and ‘A guide for critical thinkers’.

Imesha – responsible for researching ways in which the event is celebrated, including specific traditions performed to worship the Hindu god. Also, for researching the ‘Events and Ritual’ topic from week 3 to apply theory. Sources from lecture used: ‘Events and the social sciences’ (Andrews and Leopold, 2013) and ‘Rituals and traditional events in the modern world’ (Laing and Frost, 2015), used to explain the rituals and their role, specifically the ‘purification’ aspect.

Klaudia responsible for researching how the festival is celebrated in London, (particularly the Bhaktivedanta Manor, as it is an important site), the character of god Krishna, and for writing the commentary. Also, for researching the ‘Events and Community Building’ topic from week 5 to apply theory. Sources from lecture used: ‘Power, Construction and Meaning in Festivals’ (Clarke and Alan, 2017), used to explain the Hindu community that organises these events and how their values and subsequent decision making results in growth and development of the said community.

Temi responsible for researching the importance of the event and what exactly is it celebrating. Also, for ‘Cultural Identity’ (despite being a topic that we have not studied yet, we agreed it is relevant and worth exploring the subject).

Having gathered the researched and gone through the process of feedback and further improvements, all information was compiled into one shared document.

Format there was an initial conversation regarding exploring different/new formats instead of going for a traditional presentation. Having discussed the idea of a website, introduced by Klaudia, everyone has agreed, as it was different yet still practical and functional format, and was satisfied with having their own sections/articles on the site.

Website done by Klaudia – initial research and ideas gathering to develop an image of what we want the website to look like. Having some experience in HTML and CSS, yet not entirely sufficient to make everything from scratch, I decided to use a (licensed for free use) rough template as a starting point and develop our idea from there, making changes and adding stuff we needed, as well as adding the actual information we have researched and selected pictures, and testing and debugging the website during the process.
