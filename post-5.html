Celebrating the Festival in London - Bhaktivedanta Manor

Written by Klaudia

The Bhaktivedanta Manor is a self-declared ‘Spiritual Sanctuary’, a prominent and important site in London, and home to a large Hindu community. It is owned and governed by the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, also knowns as the Hare Krishna Movement (gov.uk, 2023), and is a place that regularly holds not only the Krishna Janmashtami, but any other spiritual events and gatherings as well. The manor is located in Watford, on the north-west outskirts of London, and is part of a complex site that spreads across over 78 acres of land and, additionally, consists of several gardens, a temple, a monastery, an organic farm and a protected herd of cows. It is evident that the manor community values not only the Hindu religion and celebrations, but also wellbeing and the environment, providing several support opportunities to the public (Krishna Temple, 2023a). More on the community and society in the next article.

The Krishna Janmashtami festival is held annually in August at the manor and spans across two days, having reportedly up to 50,000 attendees, and as such being the largest festival held at that site. The purpose of organising such event is to provide people the opportunity to come together, whether as family, friends or the larger community, to indulge themselves in entertaining and bonding activities, and, most notably, to celebrate the Hindu god and deepen one’s spirituality.


Subsequently, the Bhaktivedanta Manor’s event provides several attractions, including onstage entertainment programs such as music, kirtan (storytelling) and drama performances, and a sizeable bazaar giving attendees a chance to purchase various devotional items. The festival itself is very accommodating, with tickets and main (sacred and traditional) food meals being free of charge, as well as dedicated activities for children. And lastly, to explore spirituality and worship the religious god, there are several celebrative group activities, such as meditating and chanting (Krishna Temple, 2023b).

Additionally, the Bhaktivedanta Manor also organises a Shuttle Bus Service, from Stanmore Station, London, to the site, at a low price of £1, making the travel very accessible and affordable for all (Krishna Temple, 2023b).

Furthermore, there are several other Hindu Temples across London (notably the Radha-Krishna Temple) and the whole UK, that organise their own Krishna Janmashtami celebrations for the local people. However, the events are extremely similar to each other and follow the traditional ways of celebration that originated in India, and as such most of them also provide group worship, meditation and kirtan activities (ISKCON London, 2023; London Mandir, 2023; Chinmaya UK, 2023).
