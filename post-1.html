Who is Lord Krishna?

Written by Klaudia

Lord Krishna is a fundamental figure and a major god in Hinduism, believed to be the eight avatar (meaning incarnation) of Lord Vishnu, who is one of the principal deities in the Hindu pantheon. He is known as the god of love, compassion, protection and guidance, remembered by his profound and impactful teachings. Having left such mark not only in the area of spirituality, but also philosophy and Indian mythology, Krishna’s life has been documented scrupulously through various historical scriptures, such as the Bhagavad Gita and Mahabharata (PujaCraft, 2023).

Krishna’s full name in English is ‘Krishna Vasudeva’, with his first name ‘Krishna’ symbolising his blue skin colour, and ‘Vasudeva’ indicating his ancestry and lineage. The blue complexion, with which he is often depicted, has several implications and meanings in spirituality and philosophy. Firstly, it emphasises his divinity and godhood that transcendent beyond human capabilities and the mortal realm itself, and as such he is not bound nor limited by anything. Subsequently, Krishna is also associated with the cosmos, the infinite world of ever expanding sky and universal aspects of existence. Besides the sky, Krishna’s blue skin tone is also reflective of the ocean, symbolising mystery and profoundness but also the depth and intensity of his love and devotion. Those aspects, adding poetic, spiritual and romantic undertones, contributed to his continuous depictions of having blue complexity, although it still purely symbolic and not reflective of his actual physical appearance (PujaCraft, 2023).

He is described as having been a charismatic leader, a wise spiritual mentor and insightful philosopher, spreading messages of love and compassion. His involvement and role in stories depicted in aforementioned ancient scriptures continue to inspire people of all culture across the world, providing timeless guidance and wisdom important for living a fulfilling life. As such, the deity had not only had a significant impact on Indian culture and the Hindu religion, but also beyond that, inspiring several works of art as well as spiritual expressions and rituals (PujaCraft, 2023).

Lord Krishna is often depicted holding his enchanted flute, which originates from one of the stories of his childhood, whereby he was a youthful cowherd in the village of Vrindavan. His multifaceted teachings encourage righteousness and spiritual development, whilst highlighting the significance of devotion and duty (PujaCraft, 2023).
