Why is the Krishna Janmashtami Festival Celebrated?

Written by Temi

Krishna Janmashtami Festival is celebrated in more than 650 centres across the globe. The reason why Krishna Janmashtami is being celebrated is to highlight the rich Indian culture and to direct people to live through the observation of principles such as the significance of fulfilling one's duties without focusing much on the outcome. The Krishna Janmashtami Festival is being celebrated by the Lord Krishna birth which took place in Vrindavan India more than 5,000 years ago (Sharma, 2023).

Lord Krishna is considered a God of all gods. The day is meant to celebrate his life which takes place on day eight (ashtami) of the dark fortnight between August and September (Bhadrapada). Individual fasts until midnight on this day,after which a proper feast in Lord Krishna honour is done.
